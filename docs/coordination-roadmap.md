## Decisions (the resolved stances)

1. CARDINALITY IS A CLAIM, NOT A KERNEL FLAG. The existing `schema/cardinality` claim (cnf_coord schema.clj L17-20 / def-predicate! L61) becomes the SOLE runtime authority for single-ness. This kills the two-source divergence (runtime kernel list vs per-predicate claim) that is structurally isomorphic to the two-budgets bug. Grounded fact: the .bclj schema already moved to claim-only; the .clj commit path is the lone straggler.

2. COEXIST-ELECT IS THE DEFAULT CONTENTION REGIME. Rival claims to a contended (subject,predicate) COEXIST as multi; the winner is DERIVED at read time by a deterministic election (earliest-cid, tie-break lowest-agent-id) that every reader computes identically with zero coordination. No writer blocks, no writer is rejected; the loser sees itself lose on its next read and yields. `select-main-1` (today `(first cids)`, resolve.clj:100-104) becomes `elect(view,cids)`; the default election equals first-by-cid equals byte-identical to today until a real rival or override exists.

3. THE DISQUALIFIED OCC IS THE CLIENT GLOBAL-VERSION CAS, NOT PER-(s,p) CARDINALITY. The fram engine ALREADY rejects only single-valued stale writes per-(subject,predicate) (commit! `(and single (> bv base))`, bv = per-(te,p) base-version); multi-valued always appends. The real cargo-cult is the verbatim-duplicated CLIENT `assert!` that reads the GLOBAL `:version`, passes it as the per-claim base, and retries. Per-(s,p) declared-single is the substrate's own endorsed minimal axiom (VIEWS section 3), RETAINED as a narrow opt-in, never a port of SQL/Datomic's unremovable single cell.

4. THREE CONTENTION REGIMES, RANKED, EACH A PER-PREDICATE `contention` CLAIM: coexist-elect (default) > declared-single (only where duplicate work is genuinely destructive) > fenced-lease (ONLY for out-of-graph, non-idempotent side effects). The discriminator is ONE question: does the contended thing live in the graph? In-graph -> coexist-elect or declared-single; out-of-graph non-idempotent effect (build dir, external API, money) -> fenced-lease behind a documented 3-part gate (out-of-graph AND non-idempotent AND a stale holder could still be acting).

5. THREE CARDINALITY-TYPED WRITE VERBS REPLACE THE GLOBAL CAS RITUAL: append! (multi — one op, no base, no retry, coexistence free), put! (single LWW — one op, no base), swap! (single CAS — the ONLY base+retry verb, near-zero production callers after this roadmap). Make engine `:base` OPTIONAL with a one-token change (`(and single base (> bv base))`); the id-collision / reserved-predicate rejection is base-independent and untouched.

6. EVERYTHING COUNTABLE IS A FOLD OVER AN APPEND-ONLY LOG, NEVER A MUTATED CELL. Budget = Sigma(@run cost_usd); barrier = count-distinct(done_worker) >= K; liveness/online = count over fresh @run records; any concurrency ceiling = count(live drivers). Quorum (count-distinct) and Budget (Sigma) are ONE incremental-aggregate primitive with two reducers — the completion DUAL of mutual exclusion, and the proof that the completion half of coordination needs no write-time convergence.

7. KEEP EXACTLY ONE WRITER; REJECT MULTI-MASTER LOG MERGE. The single :7977 writer's only irreducible job is identity allocation (distinct things to distinct ids — the one forced substrate-global axiom). Under coexist-elect it does almost no DATA convergence (rival writes both land; convergence is a reader-side election). The bottleneck mislabeled 'single writer' is actually O(history) head re-materialization (whole-log re-fold on boot and on every out-of-band append). Fix THAT with first-class snapshots + tail-fold + incremental aggregates; logoot keys order forms-within-a-module, not a replicated log, so multi-master buys nothing append-only+OCC does not already give.

8. STATUS IS DERIVED, NEVER SET. Delete concern `set-single!` (concern-cli.clj L49). Status/maturity becomes a monotone fold over append-only `reached` claims (max over the maturity lattice); double-report is idempotent; full history is retained; cancellation is a terminal `abandoned`/`withdrawn` claim, never a status downgrade or a retract-then-assert.

9. RETRACTION IS A FIRST-CLASS, ATTRIBUTABLE, REVERSIBLE CLAIM-ABOUT-A-CLAIM (`withdrawn_by`/`withdrawn_at`/`withdrawn_reason` on the cid subject), with add-wins/remove-wins selected per VIEW by the same causal comparison that drives election — replacing the anonymous, unqueryable `cnf-supersedes` marker as the cancellation surface (the supersedes marker survives only as the internal live-fold mechanism).

10. PREDICATES ARE FIRST-CLASS @pred:* ENTITIES carrying cardinality/value_kind/doc as claims, with `same_as` alias edges so a content-addressed rename never orphans history (resurrecting bridge.clj's Predicate/override-of model). The registry is the substrate the cardinality keystone reads from; it is registered ONCE in s/setup!, never inline-minted per-CLI; a `pred lint` CI guard fails on any predicate-position string literal lacking a registry entry.

11. PORT PARTITION IS A P0 DATA-INTEGRITY BUG, FIXED FIRST AND INDEPENDENTLY. Presence currently registers to a SEPARATE live fram daemon on :7978 (harness.ts:118 hardcodes 7978 despite an existing PORT constant; bin/lodestar-on-spawn:20) that concern/roster/board (all :7977) never read, so presence silently strands. Point both at the canonical :7977 before any other work; verify with a roster smoke that SDK- and hook-spawned agents both appear.

12. THE LEASE SURVIVES ONLY FOR EXTERNAL RESOURCES. The `@lease:<thread>` driver/work-claim/exclusive-role lease family is DELETED — it is graph-internal and collapses onto coexist-elect (default) or declared-single (if dup is destructive) claims. `acquire-lease!`/`release-lease!`/`fence-ok?` and lease-cli survive ONLY for build-dir / external-API exclusion, enforced by a CI grep proving no graph-internal subject appears under `@lease:`.

# Lodestar-Native Coordination Roadmap

One opinionated, dependency-sequenced plan that collapses ~40 coordination primitives onto a single claim-native model. The thesis: **coordination is a read-time derivation over an append-only claim log, not a set of write-time locks.** Where you can elect, you don't block; where you can sum, you don't mutate; where you can derive, you don't store.

## The central resolution (the lock question, settled)

The brief's three candidates — kernel-cardinality-flag vs cardinality-as-claim vs coexist-and-elect — are not rivals at the same layer. The decisive synthesis:

1. **Cardinality is a CLAIM, not a kernel flag.** The `schema/cardinality` claim already exists and the `.bclj` schema already treats it as authoritative; the `.clj` commit path (`cnf_coord.clj` L128/L167) is the lone straggler still ORing the kernel `ck/single?` list. **Delete that OR-arm; demote the kernel list to a bootstrap seed.** Single-ness becomes view-relative and disputable for free.

2. **Coexist-elect is the DEFAULT runtime regime.** Rival claims coexist as multi; the winner is a deterministic read-time election (earliest-cid, then lowest-agent-id) every reader agrees on with zero messages. `select-main-1` becomes `elect(view, cids)`; default == `(first cids)` == byte-identical to today.

3. **The OCC the yardstick disqualified is the CLIENT global-version CAS, not per-(s,p) cardinality.** The engine already rejects only single-valued stale writes per-(subject,predicate). Per-(s,p) `declared-single` is the substrate's own endorsed minimal axiom — RETAINED as a narrow opt-in for genuinely destructive duplication. The cargo-cult is the duplicated client `assert!` that reads the GLOBAL `:version` and retries — **deleted.**

4. **Fenced leases survive ONLY for out-of-graph, non-idempotent effects** (build dir, external API). The discriminator is one question: does the contended thing live in the graph? In-graph -> elect/declared-single; out-of-graph -> lease.

Regime is itself a per-predicate `contention` claim. Purity ranking: **coexist-elect > declared-single > fenced-lease.** Every dependent design is made consistent with this.

## Dependency DAG (acyclic; the designs' circular self-claims are broken here)

```
A (port + coord + registry)
└─> B (cardinality-as-claim + coexist-elect)   [THE KEYSTONE]
     ├─> C (OCC write verbs)
     │    └─> D (snapshot + as-of + aggregates)
     ├─> E (views-as-claims)            [needs B,C]
     ├─> F (quorum)                     [needs B,D]
     ├─> G (budget=sum)                 [needs A,B,D]
     ├─> H (causality + retraction)     [needs B,E,C,D]
     ├─> I (command-as-claims)          [needs A,C,E,F,H]
     └─> J (concern footprint)          [needs A,B,C,D,H]
```

Note: every design in the input declared itself "the keystone" and depended on its siblings circularly. As lead architect I break those cycles decisively: **A is the root, B is the single keystone, and the engine-internal complements (C, D) precede the read-side generalization (E) and the derived primitives (F, G) which precede the causal refinement (H) and the two top-of-stack applications (I, J).**

## How each cross-cutting tension is resolved

- **(a) coexist-elect vs assert-if-absent-under-OCC** -> coexist-elect is DEFAULT; assert-if-absent (declared-single) is a narrow opt-in claim. Settled in B, mechanized in C, generalized in E, given a causal election variant in H.
- **(b) cardinality-as-claim vs kernel flag** -> claim wins outright; view-relative via E; registry-backed via A.
- **(c) global-version OCC is universal** -> true only in the CLIENT; deleted in C. Engine OCC is already per-(s,p) and is relocated (not deleted) to the merge-to-main decision in E.
- **(d) read-amplification of derive-everything** -> answered structurally by D (snapshots + incremental aggregates + as-of bounded by snapshot floor); B/E/F/G/H all route their O(history) folds through D.
- **(e) the single-writer funnel** -> kept deliberately (one id-allocator), defended in D; the mislabeled bottleneck (re-fold) is the actual fix.

## Sequencing guidance for execution

Ship **A first and independently** (the port fix is a P0 data-integrity bug; the coord substrate + registry are pure plumbing). Then **B** as the single flip-day change (seed-assert cardinality claims AND delete the OR-arm in ONE tx — never two deploys). **C and D** are engine-internal and can proceed in parallel once B lands. **E, F, G** fan out on the read side. **H** rides on E+C+D. **I and J** are the applications that consume the whole stack. The aggregate facility (D) MUST precede the counter deletions (G, F) so budget/quorum reads are never momentarily O(history).

# Red-team: roadmap holes & sequencing fixes

Verdict up front: the *diagnosis* is excellent and the cited facts are real (I confirmed `cnf_coord.clj:128/167` OR-arms, `resolve.clj:100-104` `select-main-1`=`(first cids)`, `concern-cli.clj:49` `set-single!`, `harness.ts:118` + `lodestar-on-spawn:20` hardcoding `7978`, and `claim-cli.clj:27-28` reading the global `:version` and passing it as per-claim `:base`). But the *plan* deletes less than it claims, smuggles OCC back in three load-bearing spots, mis-orders its real keystone, and reintroduces the exact two-source divergence it was built to kill. Specifics:

## 1. It mostly RENAMES the cargo-cults; three "deletions" are relocations

- **The kernel single-list is not deleted — it's relocated, and deleting the runtime OR-arm is an active regression.** `cnf_coord.clj:84-87` (`ensure-single-cardinality!`, called at `:152`) exists *specifically* (finding #12) to catch a single predicate that was never `def-predicate!`'d: on first write it reads `ck/single?` and pins the store's cardinality to `single` so the schema layer supersedes instead of accumulating duplicate live values. Decision 1 says "demote the kernel list to a one-time bootstrap SEED in `s/setup!`." But a seed only covers predicates known *at setup time*. Any single-valued predicate first minted **at runtime after setup** (exactly the migration case finding #12 was written for) now defaults to `multi` and silently accumulates duplicates. So the `ck/single?` OR-arm at L128/L167 isn't cargo — it's a live safety net. Deleting it requires a replacement "infer-single-on-first-write" rule the roadmap never specifies. As written, B trades a real bug-fix for a flip-day regression.
- **`cnf-supersedes` is explicitly retained** ("survives only as the internal live-fold mechanism," decision 9). That's a relabel of role, not a deletion. Fine — but stop calling it deleted.
- **The lease mechanism is 100% retained.** `acquire-lease!`/`release-lease!`/`fence-ok?` all survive; only the *subjects* are scoped out, and a **new CI grep** is added to police the convention (decision 12). That's scope-narrowing plus a new lint, not "the lease family is DELETED." Net primitive count of the lock family barely drops: the retry *ritual* goes, the lock *semantics* persist under the name `declared-single` + `swap!` + `fenced-lease`.

## 2. The lock question is NOT settled — OCC comes back in three places, two load-bearing

- **`swap!` is OCC, retained by name, with zero exercised callers.** Decision 5 keeps `swap!` as "the ONLY base+retry verb" and decision 5's engine change keeps `(and single base (> bv base))`. That is literally OCC. Worse: I verified that *no current caller threads a correct per-(s,p) base* — `claim-cli.clj:27` does a separate `{:op :version}` round-trip and passes the **global** seq as `:base` (`base-version`, `cnf_coord.clj:62-63`, is per-`(te,pid)`). The global base is coarser-but-safe, so it's not a correctness bug — but it means the per-(s,p) CAS axiom the roadmap "RETAINS as the substrate's endorsed minimal axiom" has **no correctly-exercised user today**. You're preserving a lock primitive you cannot point to one real consumer of. "Narrow opt-in" with an empty user set is a smell: name the concrete destructive-duplication case that needs `swap!`, or cut it and let `declared-single` (LWW `put!`) carry the weight.
- **Views-as-claims (E) relocates OCC to a *harder* place.** E "leaves OCC alive only at the explicit merge-to-main." Merge is inherently multi-key / cross-`(s,p)`; a single-`(s,p)` CAS doesn't compose into an atomic multi-claim merge without a transaction or lock spanning keys. So E swaps one per-key CAS for a *multi-key atomic merge* — a bigger lock than the one B deleted. Unaddressed.
- **Bootstrapping cardinality needs the very lock it demotes.** Seeding the `cardinality`/`contention`/`value_kind`/`same_as` meta-predicates must be assert-*if-absent* (don't clobber a runtime override) — and assert-if-absent **is** `declared-single`. The meta-predicates that decide regimes cannot themselves be governed by a claim-about-their-own-regime without a fixpoint; they must be hard-seeded with an invariant the roadmap never states. This is a real bootstrap circularity, not a detail.

And the regime that makes all this "coordination-free" — **coexist-elect — secretly depends on the single writer.** "Every reader elects identically with zero coordination" is true only because `earliest-cid` is a *total* order, which exists only because the single `:7977` writer allocates cids monotonically (`commit!` under `(locking (:lock co) …)`, `cnf_coord.clj:123`). Decisions 2 and 7 are load-bearingly coupled and the doc treats them as independent: the moment you shard id-allocation (which D flirts with), `earliest-cid` stops being a total order and the election needs a tiebreak that *is* coordination. State the coupling: **coexist-elect is sound iff exactly one cid allocator.**

Finally, coexist-elect's "the loser sees itself lose on its next read and yields" silently assumes **all electable work is effect-free until the winner is confirmed.** There is no stated obligation that a loser must re-read-and-confirm *before* taking any irreversible action. Election is eventually-consistent; an agent that acts on the optimistic "I'm the winner" before its next read is a real-world lost update. The model needs an explicit "don't act on an election you haven't re-confirmed past your own write" rule, or it just moved the lost-update from the DB into the agents.

## 3. Sequencing: the real keystone (D's read path) is mis-tiered behind B; A bundles a P0 with a research project

- **D should partly precede B, not follow it.** B turns every single-head read from `(first cids)` — O(1), `resolve.clj:104` — into an `elect(view, cids)` scan over a possibly-multi group, i.e. a fold. The roadmap itself admits "B/E/F/G/H all route their O(history) folds through D." If election *creates* the read-amplification and D is what makes folds cheap, then **D's snapshot + tail-fold read path is a prerequisite for B being performant**, not a Tier-2 follower. Shipping B before D risks a performance cliff on flip-day. At minimum, split D: D-read (snapshot + incremental aggregate) lands *with or before* B; D-compaction can follow.
- **Split A: the P0 port fix must not ride with the registry.** Decision 12 says ship the port fix "FIRST AND INDEPENDENTLY," but thread A and the tier table fuse port-fix + wire-helper dedup + the `@pred:*` registry (with `same_as` aliasing and a `pred lint` CI guard — a multi-week design) into one node, and B `dependsOn` all of A. So the one-line data-integrity fix (`7978`→`7977` in two files) is transitively gated behind registry design. Make **A0 = port fix (today)**, A1 = substrate/registry.
- **H is largely vacuous given C.** H "reuses OCC's discarded `:base`" to stamp causal context — but C makes `:base` *optional* and the dominant `append!` verb carries none. By the time H wants causal stamps, the majority of claims have no base, so `select-causal-1` has nothing to order on but cid → it degenerates to B's `earliest-cid` election. Either C retains a **mandatory cheap logical clock** (not the OCC base) for H to read, or H mints its own field — in which case the "reuse the discarded base" story is false. Pick one.

## 4. New cargo-cults / couplings it introduces

- **D's snapshot + incremental aggregate IS a second source of truth for the head** — structurally identical to the "two budgets" / "kernel-list vs cardinality-claim" divergence the whole roadmap exists to kill (decision 6: "never a mutated cell"). A cached `sum`/`count_distinct` that updates out-of-band from the authoritative fold is a mutable cell in a derived-aggregate costume. D must ship with an explicit contract: *snapshot is a pure, checksummed function of a log prefix; recomputable; a divergence from a tail re-fold is a loud error.* Without that, D re-commits the original sin at the aggregate layer.
- **`elect(view, cids)` is a new un-versioned consensus surface.** Every reader must compute it byte-identically *forever*. `(first cids)` had that property trivially; the moment elect takes a `view` + tie-break logic that can evolve, a rolling deploy / agent-version skew yields two elect()s → split-brain reads with **no detection** (each reader is locally consistent). Stamp an `elect-version` in the view claim and make readers refuse to elect under a view whose version they don't implement.
- **F and G are coupled to D at the *correctness* layer, not just perf.** Quorum (F) and budget (G) are admit/reject **gates**. A stale aggregate that lags the log will admit work past a budget/quorum boundary (and an agent that `append!`s a cost then immediately reads `remaining()` may not see its own write → over-admit). So D's cache needs read-your-writes consistency on the gate path — the roadmap files D as perf and F/G as semantics; they're the same concern.
- **The contention/cardinality/registry triangle is a mutually-referential bootstrap** with no specified seed-order invariant (see §2). That's new coupling A↔B↔(contention claim).
- **Internal inconsistency on where cardinality lives:** decision 1 makes the existing `schema/cardinality` claim the *sole* authority; decision 10 makes the `@pred:*` registry entity "the substrate the cardinality keystone reads from." Two homes for cardinality = the two-source divergence re-created at the meta level. Name the single authoritative home.

## 5. Still missed

- **Retraction (H) breaks quorum's monotonicity (F).** F asserts `count_distinct(done_worker)` is "monotone, idempotent, no OCC." H makes retraction first-class; a withdrawn `done_worker` **decrements** the count → the barrier can *un-fire* after agents acted on it crossing K. F and H directly contradict. Resolve explicitly: either completion predicates are irretractable (then retraction isn't universal), or quorum adopts remove-wins and loses monotonicity (then "no coordination needed" is gone — you can cross K, spend, then drop below K).
- **Compaction silently makes retraction irreversible and as-of partial.** Decision 9 promises *reversible* retraction; D compacts history below a snapshot floor and bounds as-of by that floor. A withdrawn claim compacted away can't be un-withdrawn (bytes gone) and can't be read as-of. Compaction policy must pin: never compact a claim that is the target of a live `withdrawn_*` edge or reachable from any retained as-of horizon.
- **Entity resolution / duplicate *subjects* has no election.** Coexist-elect resolves rivals on the *same* `(s,p)`. It does nothing when two parallel agents mint two subject ids for the same concept — and the single writer *guarantees they stay distinct*, which here is the wrong guarantee. `same_as` (decision 10) is scoped only to predicate renames. With many agents capturing threads, duplicate-subject proliferation is the dominant real contention, and the model has no read-time merge for it.
- **The write funnel's actual ceiling — fsync throughput — is untouched.** Decision 7 reframes the bottleneck as O(history) re-fold and fixes that with D, then declares the writer does "almost no data convergence." But every `append!` still serializes through `(locking (:lock co) …)` and a per-tx fsync (`cnf_coord.clj:123`, `:156`). Coexist-elect removes *retries* (good) but the single fsync-serialized critical section is the real cap for the "N live drivers, everything is an append" workload the roadmap envisions. No measurement, no sharded-append story, no group-commit. Don't claim the funnel is a non-issue without a sustained-append-rate number.
- **No clock model.** Liveness/online = "count over **fresh** `@run` records," the surviving fenced-lease needs a TTL + stale-holder check, and as-of/`done_at`/`sent_at`/`withdrawn_at` are wall-clock from distributed agents. Skew makes "fresh," as-of, and lease-staleness **non-deterministic across readers** — breaking the "every reader computes identically" guarantee for any time-windowed derivation. The roadmap mixes logical order (cid, clean) with physical time (dirty) and never declares which governs. The one surviving lease quietly drags this whole unspecified-clock problem with it.

## Punch-list to fix the DAG

1. Ship **A0 (port fix) alone, today**; demote registry to A1.
2. Add **D-read (snapshot + tail-fold + incremental aggregate, checksummed/recomputable) as a co-requisite of B** — election without cheap folds is a perf cliff.
3. Before deleting the L128/L167 OR-arm, specify the **infer-single-on-first-write** replacement, or you reopen finding #12.
4. Make the **single-cid-allocator ⇒ coexist-elect** dependency explicit; hard-seed the meta-predicates with a stated seed-order invariant.
5. Resolve **F↔H** (retraction vs monotone quorum) and **C↔H** (optional base vs causal stamp) before either lands.
6. Either justify `swap!` with a concrete destructive-duplication caller or cut it; give D a "divergence = loud error" contract; version `elect`.
